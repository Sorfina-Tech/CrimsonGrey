Research

Summary

The Molecular Modelling & Bioinformatics Research group is focused in many research subjects to name a few such as Oil & Gas, Health Sciences, taking advantage of an extensive know-how and expertise on molecular modelling techniques such as Molecular Dynamics Simulations, Quantum Mechanics calculations and Molecular Docking.

INTERLOCKED STRUCTURES

Molecular docking is one of the most frequently used methods in Quantitative Structure Affinity Relationship (QSAR), due to its ability to predict the binding-conformation of small molecule ligands to the appropriate target binding site






Current Research: Interlock and Molecular Docking Study of β-cyclodextrin-Guest Complex for Heavy Metal Removals







DENSITY FUNCTIONAL THEORY (DFT)



Density Functional Theory is a computational quantum mechanical modelling method used in physics, chemistry and materials science to investigate the electronic structure of many-body systems, in particular atoms, molecules, and the condensed phases.




Current Research: Metal Organics Framework-Ionic Liquids Hybrid Material for Energy Storage









SOLVATION FREE Energy

Solvation free energy of various chemicals that are dangerous to handle experimentally or too expensive to purchase can be predicted using Bennet Acceptance Ratio (BAR) method performed under Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulation.The predicted degree of acid gases removal in liquid systems can be measured and quantified by calculating the excess chemical potential (μex) and Henry’s law constant (kH).



Current Research : Free Energy Solvation of Acidic Gases such as Carbon Dioxide, Hydrogen Sulfide




MOLECULAR DYNAMICS (MD) SIMULATION

Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulation and physical analysis were employed to reveal the characteristics and the influence of ionic liquids (ILs) on the structural properties of molecules. The understanding of the properties of IL–molecule complex can be used as basis information for future development of specific ILs for specific technologies


Current research : Stability and Flexibility of β-cyclodextrin-Guest Complex Mechanism of ASP/SP In Oil-water Interface from Molecular Dynamics (MD) Simulation
